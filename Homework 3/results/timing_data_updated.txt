For this problem size, the serial version was faster than both MPI and OpenMP. I think this happened because N = 100,000 is small enough that the actual PI calculation finishes very quickly. The overhead of starting MPI processes, creating OpenMP threads, and combining partial sums costs more time than the work saved by parallelizing the loop. As the process/thread count increased, the runtime generally became worse, especially for MPI, because the overhead increased while the amount of work per process became smaller.
